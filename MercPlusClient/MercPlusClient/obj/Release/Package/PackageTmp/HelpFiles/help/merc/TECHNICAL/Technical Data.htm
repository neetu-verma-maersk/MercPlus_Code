Database Tables and Their Dependencies

COMPANY - The business responsible for receipt, tracking, approval and payment of workorders.
Dependent entries: None

CUSTOMER - The company currently responsible for equipment being repaired.
Dependent entries: None

CUSTOMER SHOP - An association of a customer to a repair shop.
Dependent entries: CUSTOMER, SHOP

CUSTOMER SHOP MODE - An association between CUSTOMER, SHOP and MODE. It is used for Non STS Modes to identify non standard time billing that will need approvals from a different security profile set up specifically for Non STS Approvers. It is also used to define a PAYAGENT associated with each combination for proper payment of a workorder in RRIS system.
Dependent entries: CUSTOMER, SHOP, MODE

EQUIPMENT LIMIT - A pricing limit for repairs on refurbished and non-refurbished equipment.
Dependent entries: None

EQUIPMENT TYPE - A classification of equipment such as container, chassis, reefer, genset.
Dependent entries: None

INDEX - Area of Equipment being repaired. The Index helps to group Repair Codes associated to the same section of equipment.
Dependent entries: MANUAL MODE

LABOR RATE - The hourly amount charged to a Customer, by a Shop for repair work done on equipment. The Rate is determined by the type of equipment being repaired and the type of hours the mechanic worked.
Dependent entries: CUSTOMER SHOP

LOCATION - A geographical area in the world.
Dependent entries: REGION

MANUAL - A guideline to equipment repairs.
Dependent entries: NONE

MANUAL MODE - The types of equipment allowed to be repaired under a given Manual.
Dependent entries: MANUAL, MODE

MANUFACTURER - The producer of the equipment.
Dependent entries: EQUIPMENT TYPE

MODE - A grouping of similar repair requirements, either pertaining to a particular type of equipment or pertaining to a particular type of repair work.
Dependent entries: NONE

NON STS CODE - Work that was done and is being invoiced that does not include repair work to a piece of equipment.
Dependent entries: MODE

PAYAGENT - The Pay Agents defined in RRIS accounting system which determine the characteristics of a payment, such as how the payment is made and the organization responsible for making the payment. Dependent entries: NONE

PAYAGENT VENDOR - An association of Payagent and Vendor.
Dependent entries: PAYAGENT, VENDOR

PREP TIME - Maximum time that can be allocated for a given type of preparation work being done on an equipment.
Dependent entries: MODE

REGION - A defined geographical area encompassing many locations.
Dependent entries: COMPANY

REPAIR CODE - An identification of work performed, including description, maximum quantities and amounts.
Dependent entries: MANUAL MODE, INDEX, alternately SUB INDEX

REPAIR CODE EXCLUSIVE - A designated relationship between one repair codes and another, which indicates the codes that are excluded from being added to a workorder when a specific repair code is already used.
Dependent entries: MANUAL MODE, REPAIR CODE

RRIS MONTH END - Indication of current designated end of the month day for RRIS processing.
Dependent entries: NONE

SHOP - A facility doing repair work on equipment.
Dependent entries: LOCATION, VENDOR

SHOP LIMITS - The limited amount a Shop can charge for repairs on a Mode. Any workorder exceeding amount will be marked for suspension for review by management.
Dependent entries: SHOP, MODE

SUB INDEX - A smaller grouping of similar repair codes. The Sub-index helps to search for a particular Repair Code.
Dependent entries: MANUAL MODE, INDEX

SUSPEND - This entity will allow managers to flag individual repair codes they would like the system to suspend when appearing on a WO. This will allow the managers to review these particular workorders more closely.
Dependent entries: SHOP, REPAIR CODE, MODE

SUSPEND CATEGORY - These generic categories will be set up by managers to identify the reasons a WO will be suspended, such as third party involvement.
Dependent entries: SUSPEND

TRANSMIT - The WO data may or may not have to be sent to RKRP for reporting purposes, or RRIS for payment purposes. Based on the Customer the work is being done for, and the type of work being done ( the Mode) the WO information will or will not be transmitted.
Dependent entries: CUSTOMER, MODE

VENDOR - A Vendor may be responsible for many Shops in multiple locations. To be paid from RRIS, the Vendor must be set up in RRIS system, and the appropriate supplier codes generated by RRIS must be updated on the Payagent Vendor table.
Dependent entries: NONE
