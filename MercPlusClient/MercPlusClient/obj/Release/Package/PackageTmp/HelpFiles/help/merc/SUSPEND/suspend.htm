SUSPEND vs. PENDING

  • When a vendor submits an estimate - it comes into MERC+ for the local MSL to review and either approve, reject or pass on to CPH for review.
  • To facilitate the review process, MERC+ is designed to segregate ordinary estimates that can be approved with little checking from those which require closer scrutiny - those which might need correction or rejection. It does this by sorting incoming estimates into three categories
    1. PENDING - an estimate which is below the suspend level, not on Global Hunt or For Sale etc - an ordinary estimate is taken into MERC+ in PENDING status. - Pending status means there is nothing extraordinary about the estimate. There is no reason to give it close scrutiny.
      • Why would an approver want to look at 25 estimates for pre-tripping reefers - each for one hour and all exactly alike?
      • Handling PENDINGS - Estimates which are PENDING can be either:
        • Approved
        • Rejected
        • Suspended
          • The approver would "suspend" and estimate if he needs to do more research before making his decision.
          • MERC+ will require the approver to add comments regarding why the estimate is being SUSPENDED.
    2. SUSPENDED - Estimates that are "suspended" (that is - held for closer scrutiny) are separated from the PENDINGS so there is no confusion.
      • Reasons estimates SUSPEND
    3. SUSPENDED FOR GLBEMR - These estimates are for units that are over the sale limit or have been singled out by GLBEMR for various reasons.
      • Local MSL will be required to add comments about the unit (general condition, equipment supply etc.) when forwarding to GLBEMR.
