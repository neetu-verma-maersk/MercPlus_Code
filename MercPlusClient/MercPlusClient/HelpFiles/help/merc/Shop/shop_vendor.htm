SHOP and VENDOR

RELATED SUBJECT - Process to set up a shop

Vendor - A vendor is the owner of a shop. A vendor can operate one or multiple shops.

 

SHOP TYPES

  • TYPE 1 SHOP

    • This shop type is defined as a Maersk Line owned or a Maersk Line affiliated repair shop.

    • A type 1 shop is allowed to purchase spare parts directly from the manufacturer of numbered parts using the agreement of Maersk Line Copenhagen and sell them to Maersk Line Copenhagen adding a mark up as updated in shop profile, to the purchase price.

    • Apart from a few parts (parts marked “Maersk = Y” in master part table) a type 1 shop supplies all parts needed for a given repair.

    • How Type 1 shops use this table

      • Parts numbers entered are paid at list price from the master parts table with following adjustments

        •  less the Manufacturer's discount (as if Maersk was buying).

        • Plus discount/markup field at the shop level.

        • Plus import tax and/or sales tax or VAT if applicable at shop level

      • Vendor is paid for parts per above

      • Amount for same is reported to RKRP – no part numbers to be transferred

  • Shop type 2

    • This shop type is defined as a non Maersk Line owned or a non Maersk Line affiliated repair shop.

    • A type 2 shop does not deliver numbered parts for repair of reefer/gensets, instead all numbered parts are supplied from and owned by Maersk Line Copenhagen

    • How Type 2 shops use this table

      • Part numbers entered are verified against master parts table with following adjustments

        • less the Manufacturer's discount (as if Maersk was buying).

        • Plus discount/markup field at the shop level.

        • Plus tax and/or VAT if applicable at shop level

      • No amount is paid to vendor for parts reported by part number

      • Part numbers are reported to RKRP for deduction from stock and price is sent to RKRP as well.

        • This price sent to RKRP should be list less mfg’s discount but with no markup.

  • Shop type 3

    • This shop type is defined as a non Maersk Line owned or a non Maersk Line affiliated repair shop.

    • A type 3 shop is purchasing spare parts directly from the manufacturer of numbered parts and sell them to Maersk Line with a mark up or a discount as updated in shop profile.

    • Apart from a few parts (parts marked “Maersk = Y” in master part table) a type 3 shop supplies all parts needed for a given repair.

    • How Type 3 shops use this table

      • Type 3  shops – Supplying parts that they purchase (exception are mandatory Maersk parts – explained below)

      • Part numbers entered are paid at list price from this table, adjusted by

        • Discount/markup field at the shop level

        • Plus tax and/or VAT if applicable at shop level.

      • Vendor is paid for parts per above

      • Amount for same is reported to RKRP – no part numbers to be transferred.

      • A type 3 shop is similar to type 1 except they do not enjoy the Manufacturer's discount.

  • Shop type 4

    • This shop type is defined as a non Maersk Line owned or a non Maersk Line affiliated repair shop.

    • A type 4 shop does not deliver numbered parts for repair of reefer/gensets, instead all numbered parts are supplied from and owned by the local Maersk Line agency (MSL).

    • Apart from a few parts (parts marked “Maersk = Y” in master part table) MSL supplies all parts needed for a given repair.

    • How Type 4 shops use this table

      • Type 4  shops – Using parts supplied by local MSL agent (exception are mandatory Maersk parts)

      • Part numbers entered are paid at list price from this table, adjusted by

        • Discount/markup field at the shop level

        • Plus tax and/or VAT if applicable at shop level.

      • Local MSL agent is paid for parts per above

      • Amount for same is reported to RKRP – no part numbers to be transferred.
